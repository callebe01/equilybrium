A note on what we watch,
and what we never will. Plainly, without footnotes, and without asterisks.

Equilybrium measures the shape of your working day. It learns the rhythm. The cadence. The quiet drift between focus and fatigue. It does not read a single word you type, say, or save. What follows is the full list, kept as short as honesty allows.

I · Here Your machine Raw activity is processed on your computer, in memory, and never written to our servers.
4h 23m · Figma 12 switches 3 meetings
II · There Only the counts Aggregated figures, like “four hours in Figma,” are what leave your computer. Nothing else.

§ 01What Equilybrium counts

  1. Time spent inside each application you open. aggregated hourly
  2. Switches between apps and browser tabs. count only · not destinations
  3. The hours of the day you tend to work. daily histogram
  4. The density of meetings on your calendar. counts · not titles or invitees
  5. The quiet gaps when your machine is idle. inferred from keyboard & mouse

§ 02What Equilybrium never reads

  1. The content of messages you write or receive. not sampled, not stored, not inferred
  2. The words inside any document or file. we do not touch the filesystem
  3. Screenshots or recordings of your screen. capture apis are disabled
  4. What you type, including credentials. no keystroke access, by design
  5. The URLs you visit in the browser. only that a browser was open
This agreement remains in force for as long as you use Equilybrium. Raw activity is processed on your machine, in memory. Only aggregated counts leave your computer. We commission a third party audit every spring and publish the report in full. Read the technical summary ›
